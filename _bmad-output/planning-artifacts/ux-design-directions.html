Long-term outcomes of cementless total hip arthroplasty in patients under 50

Muller K, et al. JBJS 2024
Abstract
This prospective cohort study evaluated 342 patients under 50 years of age who received cementless total hip arthroplasty with a minimum 10-year follow-up period. Implant survivorship was 96.2% at 15 years. Harris Hip Scores improved significantly from a mean of 42.3 preoperatively to 91.7 at final follow-up. Revision surgery was required in 13 cases (3.8%), primarily due to polyethylene wear and osteolysis.
AI Reasoning
AI Analysis High relevance to subject device. This study provides long-term clinical evidence for cementless hip arthroplasty outcomes in young patients, directly applicable to clinical evaluation of equivalent devices under MDR Annex XIV. Sample size (n=342) and follow-up duration (10+ years) meet MEDDEV 2.7/1 Rev. 4 requirements for clinical evidence.
Source Quote
"Cementless fixation in patients under 50 demonstrated excellent long-term survivorship comparable to older cohorts, supporting the use of these implants in younger, more active populations."
